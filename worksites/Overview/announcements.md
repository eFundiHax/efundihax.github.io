## About second opportunities and sick notes
**BRUNO LETARTE** — 2026-02-25

Hi everyone, In our class of almost 700 students, a class test is a big deal involving lots of people and taking lots of time. Aside from the problem selection and printing of over 5000 pages, presenting it requires 2 lecturers and 15 invigilators in 2 venues, working after regular teaching hours, accommodating people with slightly different availabilities (sports, practicals from other classes, etc). Marking all of this involves the two lecturers and around 10 markers for almost a week, while the regular stuff is still going on for the week. It’s a lot of pressure for everyone involved. In case it needs saying, it’s important to remember that what we offer one student needs to be offered to all students . We cannot have one student write yesterday’s test today, or magically create another test of equal difficulty for that student to write this weekend. This doesn’t work at the scale we are at in the class, and we have a better system to handle such problems. We chose the dates for class tests and manually booked the venues in January to secure them for our needs. It is not something that we can do at the last minute, or change/add because someone was not feeling well today. Also, the dates are linked to tutorials, and how far we are in the chapters, etc. We are offering you 3 class test opportunities this semester, but you are required to write only 2 of them . You don’t have to give us any reason for missing one, no sick note, no explanation, nothing. Hundreds of you will be missing one of these 3 opportunities this semester, it’s all good, no need to email us to say you are sorry. We made it like that to simplify everyone’s life, you can miss one, without penalty . During the mid-semester assessment period, you will write a semester test , different from class tests as it is administered by the exam office. There is only one opportunity for it, so if you have a valid, documented reason for missing it, give us the paper evidence and you will be exempted. You will NOT be given a second opportunity for the semester test , they don’t offer it. Being exempt means that everything else will scale up proportionally in weight for your marks, as if the semester test vanished completely for you. The exams: you will be given 2 opportunities to write the exam on two different dates. Every student who qualifies to write the exam is allowed to write the first, the second, or both if they want. The last one written is the one that counts, even if you get a lower mark than your first one. Sick notes are not needed, you can’t be exempt from both exams, you must write at least one to have a chance of passing. I hope this clarifies everything.
